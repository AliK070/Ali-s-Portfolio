Custom PC:

Once I finally picked up a career, I decided to use that money to build my own custom gaming computer. It can various games, allows me to render videos or simulations very quickly, and allows me to program more effectively. I reaserched and scavanged through multiple sites and vidoes, gathering the best price to performance ratio for this build, coming around ~$1000. Its an all AMD build, featuring:

  • Ryzen 5600x(6c/12t) | CPU

  • RX 6750xt (12gb GDDR6 Vram) | GPU

  • 32GB of 3600MHz | RAM

  • 700W GOLD | PSU

  • 1 TB NVME | SSD

Discriminant Calculator:

In Mathematics, the discriminant is the part of the quadratic formula underneath the square root symbol: bÂ²-4ac. It tells us how many solutions there can be (ranges from 2 to 0 solutions). As a simpler project, I decided to program a discriminant calculator to make things easier. It features a solving system, options menu, and saves your settings too. Programmed in C# using the .NET Framework and the Windows Forms graphical library. Its features are:

  • A display to show your inputed numbers, which do update.

  • Settings menu to change theme, enable graph, and more.

  • Simple but dirty saving system for your settings.
